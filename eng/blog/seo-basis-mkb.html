SEO basics for SMEs: how to rank higher on Google in 7 steps

February 2026 7 min read Sem van Wijk

SEO stands for 'Search Engine Optimization'. It's everything you do to appear higher on Google when someone searches for words related to your business. This article isn't a technical story, but 7 concrete steps any business owner can take.

Many entrepreneurs immediately think of complicated technical tricks when they hear SEO. Fortunately, reality is much simpler. At its core, SEO is about one question: does your website show exactly what your potential customer is searching for at that moment? Get that right and you'll be found; ignore it and you stay invisible, no matter how beautiful the website. The seven steps below you can largely do yourself, without hiring a specialist.

Step 1: Think about what people search for

Before doing anything about SEO, you need to know which words you want to be found for. A painter from Delft, for example, wants to be found for 'painter Delft', 'exterior painter Delft', and 'window frame painting Delft'.

Tip: Type your service + place name into Google and see what suggestions appear. Those suggestions are real search queries.

Step 2: Optimise your page titles

Your page title is the most important signal to Google. Not just 'Home', but something like 'Painter Delft & Surroundings | Craftsmanship at a fair price'.

Make sure the most important keyword comes first. And keep it under 60 characters.

Step 3: Write good meta descriptions

The meta description is the short text under the title in Google. That sentence convinces people to click your result instead of a competitor's.

Good example: 'Quality and honesty for interior, exterior, and renovation painting. Request a no-obligation quote directly.'

Step 4: Use headings with keywords

Google reads your page top to bottom. Your H1 (the main title), H2s, and H3s give structure. Work your keywords into them, naturally, not forced.

Step 5: Ensure local listing

For SMEs, a Google Business Profile (formerly Google My Business) is essential. Here you fill in your address, opening hours, photos, and reviews. This appears at the top right in Google when people search for your business.

Signing up is free via google.com/business.

Step 6: Collect reviews

Real customer reviews on Google are extremely powerful. They influence both your ranking and your click-through rate. Actively ask existing customers for a review.

Step 7: Build local links

Google sees links from other websites to yours as a recommendation. Try to get listed on local business sites, industry websites, and possibly local news sites.

A common mistake is trying to do everything at once and then never looking at your website again. SEO isn't a one-off job but a habit. Work through the steps calmly, start with the basics, and build slowly. One well-optimised page per month adds up to twelve strong pages after a year that bring customers to you.

How long does SEO take?

Be honest: SEO isn't a quick win. You'll notice the first improvements after 3 to 6 months. But if you're consistent, you build a foundation that lasts for years and delivers free customers.
