SMEs in Westland: how to grow online in 2026

April 2026 5 min read Sem van Wijk

Westland counts thousands of SMEs, from plumbers to hairdressers to growers. Local competition is fierce, and more and more customers look for their provider online, even for services they'd normally look for 'around the corner'. How do you make sure you stand out online? Five practical tips for Westland SMEs.

Westland has an entrepreneurial climate like no other. Short lines, hard work, and plenty of word-of-mouth. But the way customers find a business has changed dramatically in recent years. Where people used to call a friend for a recommendation, they now grab their phone and search online first. Whoever responds well to that has a huge head start over competitors still working the old way. The following five steps will get you there.

1. Be visible in local searches

People don't just type 'hairdresser' into Google. They type 'hairdresser Naaldwijk', 'plumber Maasdijk', or 'physiotherapist Wateringen'. Make sure your town and surrounding villages appear on your website.

For Westland, terms like Naaldwijk, Honselersdijk, 's-Gravenzande, De Lier, Wateringen, Kwintsheul, Maasdijk, Monster, and Poeldijk work well. Work these into your texts.

2. Activate your Google Business Profile

This is by far the biggest impact you can make, and it's free. A fully filled-in Google Business Profile (with photos, opening hours, reviews, and categories) means you appear in the 'local pack': the three businesses with a map above the regular search results.

Action: Go to google.com/business, claim your business, and fill everything in. This takes at most an hour, but pays off for years.

3. Actively collect reviews

In Westland, people are critical but also loyal. Good reviews multiply quickly through word of mouth. Ask every satisfied customer for a Google review, preferably during the last personal contact, while the impression is still fresh.

4. Collaborate with other local businesses

Among Westland SME owners, a lot of cross-pollination is possible. A gardener can work with a plumber, a photographer with a hair salon. Refer to each other's websites. This counts for SEO and generates leads.

5. Make your website mobile-first

Many Westland customers are entrepreneurs and farmers on the go. They don't search at home behind a desktop. They search on their phone in the car, in the shed, or on the work floor.

Your website must work perfectly for that in 2026: large and readable, buttons easy to tap, phone number callable with one tap. Many older Westland websites still aren't properly mobile-proof.

Bonus: make use of local events

Westland has many local events: fairs, markets, exhibitions. Write a news or blog article about this beforehand on your website ('We're at the Westland Marathon 2026'). This attracts targeted local visitors and boosts your local relevance in Google.

A real-world example

A painting company in Delft got 40% more requests within three months after a redesign focused on local SEO, mainly from Den Hoorn, Delfgauw, Nootdorp, and Pijnacker. Not because they advertised more, but because their website made clear to Google which region they targeted.

The nice thing about growing locally online is that you don't have to compete with big national players. You compete with businesses around the corner, and that's where you make the difference with attention to detail and consistency. A Westland entrepreneur with their online affairs in order almost always wins from a bigger player who doesn't know the region.

Conclusion

Growing online as an SME in Westland isn't rocket science. It comes down to: knowing what people search for, being findable for that, and making sure your website actually convinces customers. Start with the basics, be consistent, and you build an online presence that lasts for years.
